This reflection tree is designed as a deterministic system to guide users through structured self-reflection. It focuses on three psychological dimensions: locus of control, contribution, and perspective.

The first axis explores whether users perceive events as within their control or influenced by external factors. The second axis shifts focus to contribution versus entitlement, encouraging users to reflect on what they gave rather than what they expected. The third axis expands awareness from self-centered thinking to a broader perspective involving teams and stakeholders.

The system uses fixed options and predefined paths to ensure consistency and avoid ambiguity. Each response leads to a specific branch, creating a predictable and interpretable flow. Reflections are designed to encourage awareness without judgment.

The structure is inspired by concepts such as locus of control, growth mindset, and self-transcendence. If given more time, I would enhance the tree by adding deeper branching logic and more personalized reflections based on accumulated responses.
